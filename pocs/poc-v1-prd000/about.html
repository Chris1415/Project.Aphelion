About Aphelion

A small company with a long horizon.

We are sixty engineers, pilots and dreamers who believe spaceflight should begin with the view — and end with you a little changed.

Aphelion is named for the farthest point in an orbit — the moment a body is most distant from the star it circles, moving slowest, seeing most. It seemed the right name for a company built around the far view rather than the fast trip.

We began in 2031 with a single conviction: that the first generation of civilian spaceflight had optimised for the wrong thing. Everyone was racing for altitude records and minutes of weightlessness. No one was designing for the part that actually changes people — the long, quiet look back at Earth.

So we built the opposite of a thrill ride. Quiet cabins. Slow rotations. Windows the length of a bed. Crews trained as much in hospitality as in flight. And a sustainability program that balances every gram of carbon we lift, because we refuse to spend the planet in order to show it to you.

Seven destinations and two thousand guests later, the conviction has only deepened. The far point is where you see the whole. That is the only place we are interested in taking you.

What we hold to

Four fixed points.

Safety is the whole job

Every system is triple-redundant and every guest is crew-trained. A 99.97% record is not luck — it is the only number we refuse to round.

The view comes first

We design every voyage backward from the window. If a feature competes with the horizon, the horizon wins.

We balance what we burn

Reusable craft, green-methane propulsion, verified offsets. Showing people the planet should never cost the planet.

Small on purpose

A handful of guests per cycle, a crew that learns your name. Scale is the enemy of the thing we are trying to give you.
