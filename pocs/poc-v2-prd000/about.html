03 — The program

We measure first.
Then we fly.

Aphelion was founded on the belief that the most luxurious thing in flight is certainty — and that certainty is a discipline, not a feeling.

2019
First crewed window
↳— The story Founded · Cape Coast Flight Campus

Aphelion did not begin with a rocket. It began with a spreadsheet of every civilian flight that had ever gone wrong, and a refusal to add a row to it. Our founders — two flight surgeons, an avionics engineer, and a celestial navigator — were tired of an industry that sold the view and hid the numbers.

So we inverted the order. We publish the telemetry before we sell the seat. We brief for two days before we fly for one hour. We name no destination we are not certified to reach, and we cancel without apology when a margin drifts. The result is a program that feels less like an adventure company and more like an observatory that happens to take passengers.

Today, six vehicles fly from our coastal campus on a published cadence. Our passengers are scientists, artists, retired pilots, and the quietly curious — people who would rather know exactly where they are than be told it is somewhere wonderful. We think that is the highest form of wonder there is.

04— Operating principles Four, non-negotiable
PR.01

Margins before marketing

No flight is sold until its safety case is dull. If the numbers excite us, we are not done engineering them.

PR.02

The passenger sees the data

Every readout our flight directors watch is mirrored to you, live. Informed consent is a feature, not a form.

PR.03

Cancel without ego

A scrubbed window is a success, not a failure. We have never flown to keep a schedule, and we never will.

PR.04

Leave the sky as we found it

Every vehicle is recovered, every orbit logged, every object tracked. We fly as guests of a commons.
